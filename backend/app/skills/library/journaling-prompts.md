## When something's stuck
"What am I actually feeling right now, underneath the first word I reached
for?" Naming the feeling more precisely than "stressed" or "fine" often
loosens it a bit just by being specific.

## End-of-day processing
"What's one thing today that was harder than it should have been, and one
thing that went better than expected?" Balances venting with not losing
the whole day to the hard part.

## Before-bed brain dump
Listing whatever's looping — worries, tomorrow's tasks, unfinished
thoughts — without trying to solve any of it, just to get it out of your
head and onto paper. Pairs well with sleep-hygiene's note on racing
thoughts at night.

## When stuck on what to write
"If I were describing today to a close friend, what would I actually say?"
Writing toward an imagined listener often unlocks more than writing toward
a blank page.

## A note on how to offer this
Journaling suggestions land better as a light, optional nudge ("might be
worth writing down") than as homework — the value is in the person choosing
to do it, not in compliance with a suggestion.
